296. Margin Fundamentals: Purpose

Files Modified: None

We've reached the outermost layer of the box model: Margin.

  • Purpose: Margin creates space outside an element's border. Its primary function is to control the space between adjacent elements, pushing them away from each other.
  • Transparency: Margins are always transparent; an element's background color or image never extends into its margin area.
  • Collapsing: Vertical margins (top and bottom) have a unique behavior called "margin collapsing," where adjacent vertical margins combine under certain conditions. Horizontal margins do not collapse.

Margins are essential for creating gutters, spacing out sections, and generally controlling the overall layout flow and separation of elements.
