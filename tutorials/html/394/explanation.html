394. <figure> & <figcaption>: Semantics

Files Modified: None

Let's reinforce the semantic purpose of <figure> and <figcaption>.

  • Self-Contained Unit: The core idea of <figure> is that the content within it (the image, code block, etc., plus its caption) forms a single, self-contained unit that relates to the main document flow but could be moved elsewhere without disrupting that flow. Think of figures in a textbook â€“ they illustrate a point but could be placed at the end of the chapter or in an appendix.
  • Not Just for Images: While commonly used for images, <figure> is appropriate for any self-contained content needing a caption, such as diagrams, charts, videos, audio clips, code listings, or even quotes.
  • Explicit Association: <figcaption> provides an explicit, programmatically determinable link between the figure's content and its caption. This is much better semantically than just putting an image and a paragraph below it and hoping users understand the connection. Assistive technologies can announce the caption as belonging to the figure.

Using these elements correctly adds significant semantic richness to your document.
