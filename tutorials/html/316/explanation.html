Elements with display: block; are fundamental building blocks for page structure.

  • Behavior:
    1. Starts on a New Line: Block elements force a line break before and after themselves, stacking vertically in the normal document flow.
    2. Full Available Width: By default (width: auto;), they expand horizontally to fill the entire width of their containing block.
    3. Respects Dimensions/Spacing: width, height, margin (including vertical margins and auto horizontal centering), and padding are all fully respected.
  • Default Block Elements: Many common structural and content elements are block-level by default, including: <div>, <p>, <h1>-<h6>, <ul>, <ol>, <li>, <form>, <header>, <footer>, <section>, <article>, <nav>, <blockquote>, <hr>, <pre>.
