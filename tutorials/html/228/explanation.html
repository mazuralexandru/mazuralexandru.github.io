228. <em> & <strong>: HTML Recap

Files Modified: None

Let's solidify the semantic difference:

  • <em>: Changes the meaning of the sentence through vocal stress.
    • Cats are <em>cute</em>. (Implies cuteness is the key quality, maybe compared to other qualities).
    • <em>Cats</em> are cute. (Implies cats, specifically, are cute, maybe compared to dogs).
  • <strong>: Marks text as important without changing the core meaning. Often used for warnings, key terms in instructions, or labels.
    • Please read the <strong>safety instructions</strong> carefully.
    • Log in using your <strong>username</strong>.

Browsers provide default styling (italic/bold) as visual cues for these semantics, but the underlying meaning is distinct. Assistive technologies like screen readers might use different voice intonations for <em> vs. <strong>.
