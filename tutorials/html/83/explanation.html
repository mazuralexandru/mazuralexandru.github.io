83. Other Inline Semantics: Superscript (<sup>)

Complementary to <sub> is the <sup> element, used for superscript text.

The Superscript Element (<sup>)

  • Purpose: Renders text as superscript, meaning it appears slightly above the normal baseline and often in a slightly smaller font size.
  • Semantics: Represents text that should be superscript according to established typographical conventions.
  • Type: Inline element.
  • Syntax: <sup>...</sup>

Use Cases:

The semantic meaning restricts its use primarily to:

  • Mathematical Exponents: Like the '2' in E=mc².
  • Ordinal Numbers: Like the 'st' in 1st, 'nd' in 2nd, 'rd' in 3rd, 'th' in 4th.
  • Footnote/Endnote Markers: Like the '[1]' in See note[1].
  • (Sometimes used in other specific scientific notations).

Default Rendering:

Browsers raise the baseline of the text inside <sup> and may slightly reduce its font size.
