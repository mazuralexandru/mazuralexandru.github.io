51. Focusing: Removing <i> and <b> Examples

We've explored the nuances of <i> (alternate voice) and <b> (stylistic offset) and contrasted them with the more semantically strong <em> and <strong>. While <i> and <b> have their valid (though more limited) uses, <em> and <strong> are generally more common and fundamental for conveying meaning.

To simplify our example and prepare for the next set of elements (structural breaks like <br> and <hr>), we will now remove the <i> and <b> examples we added.

Why Remove Them?

  • Focus: Shift attention away from the subtle distinctions between <i>/<b>/<em>/<strong> and towards upcoming structural elements.
  • Relevance: The specific examples (technical terms, SuperWidget) are no longer needed for the immediate next steps.
  • Preview Clarity: Reduces the number of differently styled inline elements, making the remaining <em> and <strong> examples (including the nested one) stand out more clearly if needed for review, before they too are potentially pruned.
