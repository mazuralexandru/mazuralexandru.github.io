510. Landmarks: <footer> Element - Fundamentals

The <footer> element defines the footer region for its nearest sectioning ancestor or the page itself.

Definition & Purpose:

  • The <footer> element represents footer or closing content for its sectioning parent (e.g., page <body>, <article>, <section>).
  • It typically contains information about its section, such as:
    • Authorship information (<address>).
    • Copyright data (<small>).
    • Links to related documents (privacy policy, terms of service, sitemap).
    • Contact information.
    • Publication dates (within an <article> footer).

Scope & Usage:

  • Page Footer: When used as a direct child of <body>, it usually defines the main footer for the entire website, often containing site-wide links and copyright info.
  • Section Footer: Can also be used inside <article>, <section>, <aside>, or <nav> to provide concluding information specifically for that section.
  • Multiple Footers: A document can have multiple <footer> elements.

Core Mechanics:

  • Block-level element.
  • Syntax: <footer> ... </footer>
  • Content: Flow content, but cannot directly nest another <footer> or <header>. Often contains <p>, <small>, <address>, lists of links.

Accessibility:

  • When used as a top-level footer (direct child of <body>, or sometimes sectioning elements), it maps to the ARIA contentinfo landmark role. This helps screen reader users locate common site-wide information like contact details or privacy policies.

Code Change:

We've added a top-level <footer> element after the <main> element in index.html, containing typical footer content (copyright using <small>, links).

<!-- index.html snippet -->
<body>
  <header>...</header>
  <main>...</main>

  <!-- Added footer -->
  <footer>
    <p><small>&copy; 2024 Your Website Name. All rights reserved.</small></p>
    <p><a href="/privacy">Privacy Policy</a> | <a href="/terms">Terms of Service</a></p>
  </footer>

  <!-- <p>This paragraph is temporarily commented out.</p> -->
</body>

✅ Observation:

  1. Locate the new <footer> element placed after <main> in index.html. Note the use of <small> for the copyright.
  2. In the browser preview, the footer content appears below the main content area. It should also have the dashed sky blue border applied by our temporary CSS, identifying it as the contentinfo landmark region.

✨ Key Takeaway: Use the <footer> element to semantically group footer content (copyright, related links, contact info) for the page or specific sections like <article> or <section>. Top-level footers provide an important contentinfo landmark for accessibility.
