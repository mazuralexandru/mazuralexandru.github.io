12. Preparing for Structure: Clearing Examples

Before we build the correct minimal structure for an HTML document, let's clean up the examples we just added.

Why Remove Code?

  • Focus: Our next steps involve creating the foundational <!DOCTYPE>, <html>, <head>, and <body> elements. The paragraphs and breaks we added were just for demonstrating basic tag syntax. Keeping them now would clutter the view as we focus on the overall document structure.
  • Relevance: Those specific examples (<p>, <hr>, <br>) are not directly needed for understanding the core document structure itself. We'll re-introduce content elements inside the <body> later.
  • Incremental Learning: We introduce concepts (like <p>), demonstrate them, and then sometimes clear the stage to focus on the next core concept without distraction.
