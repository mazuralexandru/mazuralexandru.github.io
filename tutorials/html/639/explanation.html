639. Representing Symbols: Punctuation & Spacing (&mdash;, &ndash;, etc.)

Finally, let's look at entities commonly used for specific punctuation or spacing needs.

Common Punctuation/Spacing Entities:

  • Dashes:
    • &mdash; (—): Em dash (longer dash, often used for breaks in thought or parenthetical statements, sometimes without spaces around it).
    • &ndash; (–): En dash (mid-length dash, often used for ranges like dates or numbers, or compound adjectives). Distinct from the hyphen (-).
  • Ellipsis:
    • &hellip; (…): Represents omitted text or a trailing off thought (single character, not three periods).
  • Bullets:
    • &bull; (•): A bullet point symbol. (Though usually lists are made with <ul>/<li> which provide bullets automatically).
  • Smart Quotes / Guillemets:
    • &lsquo; (‘) / &rsquo; (’): Typographically correct single quotes/apostrophe.
    • &ldquo; (“) / &rdquo; (”): Typographically correct double quotes.
    • &laquo; («) / &raquo; (»): Angle quotation marks (Guillemets), used in some languages like French.
    • Note: While direct input of smart quotes is possible with UTF-8, using entities can ensure consistency if editor settings or copy-pasting might introduce plain straight quotes ('/").
