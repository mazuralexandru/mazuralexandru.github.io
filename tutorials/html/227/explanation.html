227. Emphasis & Importance (<em>, <strong>)

Files Modified: None

Now let's shift focus back to HTML and look at specific elements used for text semantics, starting with emphasis and importance.

  • <em> (Emphasis):
    • Semantic Meaning: Represents stress emphasis on its content. Think of how you'd change your tone of voice to emphasize a word. It changes the meaning of the sentence.
    • Default Styling: Typically rendered in italics by browsers.
    • Example: You <em>must</em> complete the form. (Emphasizing "must").
  • <strong> (Strong Importance):
    • Semantic Meaning: Represents strong importance, seriousness, or urgency for its content. Doesn't change the meaning like <em>, but highlights key information.
    • Default Styling: Typically rendered in bold by browsers.
    • Example: <strong>Warning:</strong> Do not exceed the maximum dosage. (Highlighting the warning).

Key Distinction: Use <em> for emphasis that changes meaning, and <strong> for highlighting important text. Don't choose them based purely on their default italic/bold appearance.** If you just want italics or bold for presentation without semantic meaning, use CSS (font-style: italic; or font-weight: bold;) potentially applied via a class, or consider the presentational <i> and <b> tags if appropriate (covered next).
