53. Resetting Paragraphs: Removing Simple Emphasis/Importance

To provide the cleanest possible slate for introducing the structural elements <br> and <hr>, we will now remove the remaining simple <em> and <strong> examples from our paragraphs.

Why Remove Them?

  • Complete Focus: By reverting the paragraphs to plain text, we eliminate all potential distraction from inline styling or semantics. The focus shifts entirely to the block-level structure and the upcoming elements that modify it (<br>, <hr>).
  • Clean Canvas: Provides a simple, standard paragraph structure as the baseline for observing the effects of the next elements.

Code Change:

We have removed the <em>emphasis</em> and <strong>importance</strong> tags from the first paragraph, and the <strong>second</strong> tag from the second paragraph. We've also adjusted the text slightly.

<body>
  <h1>Main Page Title (h1)</h1>
  <p>This is the introductory paragraph. We will soon explore line breaks and thematic breaks.</p> <!-- Removed em/strong, adjusted text -->
  <!-- Removed: We will add <em>emphasis</em> and <strong>importance</strong> to text. -->

  <p>This is another paragraph, serving as a second block of text.</p> <!-- Removed strong, adjusted text -->
  <!-- Removed: It serves as a <strong>second</strong> block of text for our examples. -->

  <!-- <p>This paragraph is temporarily commented out.</p> -->
</body>

✅ Observation:

  1. Verify that index.html no longer contains any <em> or <strong> tags within the two main paragraphs.
  2. The browser preview should now show the <h1> followed by two completely plain paragraphs of text (inheriting the base styles for font and color, but without any inline italics or bolding).

✨ Key Takeaway: Sometimes, the best way to prepare for a new concept is to reset the example code to a very simple baseline state, removing even relevant previous examples to ensure maximum clarity for the new topic.
