463. Reset: Preparing for <select> Element

We've covered single-line inputs (<input>) and multi-line inputs (<textarea>). Now we move to controls that allow users to choose from a predefined list of options: the <select> element, which typically renders as a dropdown list.

To focus on <select> and its child elements (<option>, <optgroup>), we will remove the <textarea> example from our form.

Why Remove Code?

  • Focus: Isolate the <select> element and its unique structure involving child options.
  • Clean Slate: Start with an empty form container.
