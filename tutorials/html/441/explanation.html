441. background-position: Four-value Syntax

Files Modified: None

The four-value syntax for background-position provides more explicit control by specifying an offset from a chosen edge.

  • Syntax: background-position: <edge-keyword> <length-percentage>? <edge-keyword>? <length-percentage>?
    • You specify a horizontal edge (left or right) followed optionally by its offset.
    • You then specify a vertical edge (top or bottom) followed optionally by its offset.
    • The order of horizontal/vertical pairs doesn't matter.
  • Examples:
    • background-position: right 20px bottom 10px; (Image is 20px from the right edge and 10px from the bottom edge).
    • background-position: top 5px left 15px; (Image is 15px from the left edge and 5px from the top edge - equivalent to 15px 5px).
    • background-position: right center; (Equivalent to right 50% or right).
    • background-position: bottom 10% left; (Aligned to the left edge, 10% up from the bottom edge).

This syntax is less common than the two-value syntax but offers precise control when aligning relative to edges other than the top-left.
