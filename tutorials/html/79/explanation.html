79. Other Inline Semantics: Small Print (<small>)

Let's move on to another category of inline semantic elements. First, we'll look at <small>.

The Small Print Element (<small>)

  • Purpose: Represents side comments, small print, or legal text like copyright notices, disclaimers, licensing information, or attributions. It indicates content that is typically rendered smaller and is somewhat tangential or supplementary to the main text.
  • Semantics: It signifies that the content is 'fine print' or auxiliary information, not just visually smaller text.
  • Type: Inline element.
  • Syntax: <small>...</small>

Default Rendering:

Browsers typically render text inside <small> tags in a smaller font size than the surrounding text.
