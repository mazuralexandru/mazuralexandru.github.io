64. Text Styling: text-transform

The text-transform property allows you to change the capitalization of text without altering the underlying HTML content.

text-transform Explained

  • Purpose: Controls the capitalization of text content.
  • Values:
    • none: (Default) No transformation is applied. Text renders as typed in the HTML source.
    • uppercase: Transforms all characters to uppercase (e.g., "text" becomes "TEXT").
    • lowercase: Transforms all characters to lowercase (e.g., "TeXt" becomes "text").
    • capitalize: Transforms the first letter of each word to uppercase and remaining letters to lowercase. (Note: The exact definition of a "word" can be complex and browser-dependent, especially with punctuation).
    • full-width: Transforms characters to be rendered within a full-width square (used mainly for East Asian scripts). Not commonly used for Latin scripts.
  • Semantic vs. Presentation: This property only affects the visual rendering. The actual text content in the HTML remains unchanged, which is important for accessibility (screen readers will read the original text) and SEO. Use it for presentational capitalization, not as a substitute for correct capitalization in your source content.

In Our Example:

  • .uppercase-text converts its content entirely to uppercase.
  • .lowercase-text converts its content entirely to lowercase, regardless of the original casing in the HTML.
  • .capitalize-text capitalizes the first letter of each word.
