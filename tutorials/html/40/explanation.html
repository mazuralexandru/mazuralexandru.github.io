40. Why No Closing Tag for Void Elements?

Files Modified: None

The fundamental reason void elements don't have (and don't need) a closing tag is that they cannot contain any content.

Elements like <p>, <div>, or <h1> are container elements. Their opening and closing tags define the boundaries of the content they hold.

Void elements like <img>, <br>, or <input> are not containers. They represent a single, self-contained entity or instruction.

  • An image (<img>) is just an image; it doesn't make sense to put text or other elements inside the image tag itself.
  • A line break (<br>) is just an instruction to break the line; there's no content within the break.

Since they can't hold content, a closing tag would be redundant and serve no purpose. The single tag is sufficient to represent the element and its function.
