496. Semantics: Why Use Semantic HTML? - Introduction to Benefits

Now that we know what semantic HTML is, let's explore why it's the standard and recommended approach. Using semantic elements provides numerous significant advantages over relying on non-semantic elements like <div> and <span> for everything.

Key Benefit Areas:

In the next few lessons, we will detail the benefits across these major areas:

  1. Accessibility (A11y): Perhaps the most crucial benefit, making web content usable for people with disabilities relying on assistive technologies.
  2. Search Engine Optimization (SEO): Helping search engines understand the structure and relevance of your content, potentially improving discoverability.
  3. Maintainability & Readability: Making your code easier for developers (including your future self) to read, understand, update, and collaborate on.
  4. Future Compatibility & Interoperability: Ensuring your content is better understood by current and future browsers, devices, and web services.
  5. (Often) Simpler Code: Reducing reliance on excessive class attributes for basic structure identification.

Understanding these benefits solidifies the importance of choosing the right HTML element for the job based on its meaning.
