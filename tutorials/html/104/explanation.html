104. Preparing for Ordered Lists: Removing <ul> Example

We have thoroughly explored the fundamentals, mechanics, styling hooks, best practices, and accessibility aspects of unordered lists (<ul>).

Now, we will transition to the second major list type: ordered lists (<ol>). To ensure a clear focus, we will first remove the <ul> example from our index.html.

Why Remove Code?

  • Focus: To introduce <ol> without the visual distraction of the previous <ul> example.
  • Clear Comparison: Starting fresh makes it easier to directly compare the behavior and appearance of <ol> with what we just learned about <ul>.
  • Clean Slate: Resets the main content area for the new list type.

Code Change:

We have removed the entire <ul>...</ul> block and its contained <li> elements. We've also updated the <h1> and paragraph text to introduce ordered lists.

<body>
  <h1>Ordered Lists (ol)</h1> <!-- Updated H1 -->
  <p>Preparing to demonstrate ordered lists.</p> <!-- Adjusted P -->

  <!-- Removed ul -->
  <!--
  <ul>
    <li>Item One</li>
    <li>Item Two</li>
    <li>Item Three</li>
  </ul>
  -->

  <p>Ordered lists are used when sequence matters.</p> <!-- Adjusted P -->

  <!-- <p>This paragraph is temporarily commented out.</p> -->
</body>

✅ Observation:

  1. Verify that the <ul> element and its list items are gone from index.html.
  2. The browser preview should now show only the new heading and the two introductory paragraphs, ready for the <ol> example.

✨ Key Takeaway: Removing the <ul> example provides a clean transition to learning about ordered lists (<ol>), allowing for focused learning and comparison.
