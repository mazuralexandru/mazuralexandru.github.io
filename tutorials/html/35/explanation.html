35. Text: Headings (<h1> to <h6>) - Fundamentals

After paragraphs, the next most important elements for structuring text content are headings. Headings create a hierarchy and outline for your document.

Heading Elements (<h1> to <h6>)

  • Purpose: Define headings for sections and subsections of a document. They signal the topic of the content that follows them.
  • Hierarchy: There are six levels of headings:
    • <h1>: The highest level, typically used for the main title of the entire page or document.
    • <h2>: Represents a major section heading.
    • <h3>: Represents a subsection within an <h2> section.
    • <h4>, <h5>, <h6>: Represent progressively lower levels of subsections.
  • Semantics: Headings establish the structure and outline of your content. They are crucial for understanding the organization of the information.
  • Type: Like paragraphs, headings are block-level elements. They start on new lines and typically have default top and bottom margins.
