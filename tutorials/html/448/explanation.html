448. background-attachment: fixed

Files Modified: style.css

Setting background-attachment: fixed; creates a parallax-like effect.

  • Behavior: The background image is positioned relative to the viewport. It remains fixed in place even when the page or the element containing it is scrolled.
  • Effect: The content scrolls "over" the stationary background image.
  • Considerations: Can sometimes have performance implications on less powerful devices, especially with large images, as the browser needs to constantly repaint the content over the fixed background during scroll. Also, be mindful of readability – ensure text scrolling over the fixed background remains legible.

✅ Observe style.css:

  • We've changed background-attachment: scroll; to background-attachment: fixed; in the body rule.
body {
  min-height: 150vh; 
  background-image: url('images/placeholder-landscape.jpg'); 
  background-repeat: no-repeat;
  background-position: center center;
  background-size: cover;
  /* background-attachment: scroll; <- Changed */
  background-attachment: fixed; /* Background fixed relative to viewport */

  background-color: #2e2e2e; 
  color: #E0E0E0; 
  font-family: sans-serif;

}
