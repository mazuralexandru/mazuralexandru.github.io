303. Margin Collapsing (Vertical Margins): Concept

Files Modified: None

One of the trickiest aspects of CSS margins is margin collapsing. This behavior applies only to the vertical margins (top and bottom) of block-level boxes in the normal document flow.

  • Core Concept: When two or more vertical margins touch or overlap, they combine (collapse) into a single margin. The size of this collapsed margin is equal to the largest of the individual margins involved, not their sum.
  • Purpose (Intended): This behavior is designed to create consistent vertical spacing between paragraphs and headings without requiring manual adjustments. If two paragraphs each have margin-bottom: 1em; and margin-top: 1em;, the space between them will be 1em, not 2em, which is usually the desired spacing.
  • Horizontal Margins: Horizontal margins (margin-left, margin-right) never collapse.

Understanding where and why margins collapse is key to debugging unexpected vertical spacing issues.
