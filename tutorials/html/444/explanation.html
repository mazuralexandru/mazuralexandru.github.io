444. background-size: Values (cover & contain)

Files Modified: style.css

The cover and contain keywords are particularly useful for responsive backgrounds.

  • cover:
    • Scales the background image (while maintaining its aspect ratio) so that it is as large as possible to completely cover the background positioning area.
    • If the image's aspect ratio doesn't match the container's, some parts of the image will be clipped (overflow the container) to avoid leaving empty space.
    • Useful for full-page background photos or ensuring a container is always filled with the image texture.
  • contain:
    • Scales the background image (while maintaining its aspect ratio) so that it is as large as possible while ensuring the entire image is visible within the background positioning area.
    • If the image's aspect ratio doesn't match the container's, there will be empty space left in the container (either horizontally or vertically).
    • Useful for displaying logos or diagrams where the entire image must be visible without cropping.
