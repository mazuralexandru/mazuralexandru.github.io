82. Other Inline Semantics: Subscript (<sub>)

Now let's look at the <sub> element, used for subscript text.

The Subscript Element (<sub>)

  • Purpose: Renders text as subscript, meaning it appears slightly below the normal baseline and often in a slightly smaller font size.
  • Semantics: Represents text that should be subscript according to established typographical conventions, primarily for mathematical or chemical notation.
  • Type: Inline element.
  • Syntax: <sub>...</sub>

Use Cases:

The semantic meaning restricts its use primarily to:

  • Chemical Formulas: Like the '2' in H₂O.
  • Mathematical Indices: Like the 'i' or 'n' in variables like xᵢ or y<0xE2><0x82><0x99>. (Often referred to as subscripts in math).

Default Rendering:

Browsers lower the baseline of the text inside <sub> and may slightly reduce its font size.

Code Change:

We've added examples of <sub> in the paragraphs of index.html for both chemical and mathematical notation.

<body>
  <h1>Other Inline Semantics</h1>
  <p>Subscript is used for things like chemical formulas: H<sub>2</sub>O.</p> <!-- Added <sub> -->

  <p>It's also used for mathematical indices, like x<sub>i</sub> or y<sub>n</sub>.</p> <!-- Added <sub> -->
