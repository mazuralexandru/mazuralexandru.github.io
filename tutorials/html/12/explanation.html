Heading Elements (<h1> to <h6>)

  • Purpose: Define headings for sections of content. They create a document outline and are crucial for structure, accessibility, and SEO.
  • Levels: There are six levels:
    • <h1>: The main heading for the entire page (usually only one per page).
    • <h2>: Subheading for a major section.
    • <h3>: Sub-subheading, and so on down to <h6>.
  • Semantic Meaning: Use heading levels according to the logical structure of your content, not just to make text look big or small. <h1> is the most important, <h6> the least. Don't skip levels (e.g., don't go from <h1> directly to <h3>).
  • Default Styling: Browsers apply default styling to headings, typically making them bold and progressively smaller from <h1> down to <h6>. They also usually have some top and bottom margin.
