52. Simplifying Further: Removing Nested Emphasis/Importance

We demonstrated nesting <em> and <strong> to show it's possible. However, for the upcoming examples focusing on line breaks (<br>) and thematic breaks (<hr>), even the simple inline emphasis/importance examples can be a slight distraction.

Let's remove the nested example (<strong><em>very</em></strong> important!) to further simplify the paragraph content.

Why Remove It?

  • Focus: To ensure maximum clarity when introducing <br> and <hr>, which affect the structure around or within paragraphs, rather than the styling of words inside them.
  • Incremental Cleanup: Continuing the process of removing examples once their primary point has been made.

Code Change:

We have removed the <strong><em>very</em></strong> important! snippet from the end of the first paragraph in index.html.

<p>This is the introductory paragraph. We will add <em>emphasis</em> and <strong>importance</strong> to text. <!-- Removed nested example --> </p>
<!-- Removed: This part is <strong><em>very</em></strong> important! -->

✅ Observation:

  1. Confirm that the nested <strong><em>...</em></strong> structure is gone from index.html.
  2. The browser preview for the first paragraph should now end after the word "text.". The bold-italic "very" and bold "important!" are removed.

✨ Key Takeaway: Continuing to prune even recently added examples helps keep the focus tight on the very next concept to be introduced.
