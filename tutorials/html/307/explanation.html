307. Margin Collapsing: Rules & Exceptions

Files Modified: None

Margin collapsing can seem complex, but here's a summary of the key rules and when collapsing doesn't happen:

Collapsing Occurs Between:

  1. Adjacent Siblings: Top margin of one block collapses with the bottom margin of the preceding block.
  2. Parent and First/Last Child: Parent's top margin collapses with first child's top margin (if no top border/padding/inline content/clearance on parent). Parent's bottom margin collapses with last child's bottom margin (if no bottom border/padding/inline content and parent height is auto).
  3. Empty Blocks: An empty block's own top and bottom margins collapse (if no border/padding/height/content).

Collapsing Does NOT Occur:

  • Horizontally: Left and right margins never collapse.
  • With Padding/Borders: If there is any padding or border separating the margins, they won't collapse (e.g., adding padding-top: 1px; or border-top: 1px solid transparent; to the parent prevents parent/child top margin collapse).
  • With Inline Content: If there is any inline content (even a non-breaking space &nbsp;) between the margins (e.g., text directly inside the parent before the first child).
  • For Floated Elements: Margins of floated elements do not collapse with other margins.
  • For Absolutely Positioned Elements: Margins of absolutely positioned elements do not collapse.
  • For Inline-Block Elements: Margins of inline-block elements do not collapse with other margins (though their own top/bottom margins don't collapse with adjacent block margins either).
  • For Elements with overflow other than visible: Setting overflow: hidden;, scroll;, or auto; on a parent prevents its margins from collapsing with its children's margins (as seen in our parent/child example).
  • For Flexbox/Grid Items: Margins of flex items or grid items do not collapse with each other or with their container margins.

Dealing with Collapse:

  • Often, collapsing is the desired behavior for paragraph spacing.
  • If it causes issues (e.g., child margin "escaping" parent), use techniques to prevent it: add minimal padding/border to the parent, set overflow on the parent, or use layout methods like Flexbox/Grid where collapsing doesn't occur.
