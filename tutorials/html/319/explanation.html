319. Deprecated <meta>: Keywords (name="keywords") - Fundamentals & History

Let's examine the <meta name="keywords"> tag. It's important to understand its history to grasp why it's no longer relevant for modern SEO.

Original Purpose:

  • In the early days of the web (1990s), search engines were less sophisticated. The keywords meta tag was introduced as a way for website creators to explicitly tell search engines which keywords and phrases were relevant to the page's content.
  • Search engines would read this list and use it as a significant factor in determining which pages to show for user queries matching those keywords.

Attribute Pairing:

  • Uses the standard name/content pair.
    • name="keywords": Identifies the metadata type.
    • content="comma, separated, list, of, relevant, keywords": Contains the list of keywords.

Historical Relevance:

  • During the 1990s and early 2000s, this tag was actively used by major search engines for indexing and ranking.

The Problem: Abuse (Keyword Stuffing)

  • Because it directly influenced rankings, the keywords tag quickly became heavily abused. Website owners engaged in "keyword stuffing" – filling the content attribute with hundreds of keywords, including irrelevant or competitor terms, in an attempt to manipulate search results.
  • This made the tag an extremely unreliable and spammy signal of page relevance.

✅ Observation: No code changes yet. Understand the original intent: to help early search engines understand page topics via a supplied keyword list. Also grasp the reason for its downfall: widespread abuse through keyword stuffing.

✨ Key Takeaway: The <meta name="keywords"> tag was originally intended to provide relevant keywords to early search engines but was heavily abused, leading to its deprecation as a significant ranking factor.
