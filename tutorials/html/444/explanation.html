444. Reset: Preparing for Labels (<label>)

We have now seen several <input> types (text, password, email, number, url, tel, date, search, checkbox, radio) and learned how to group radio buttons using <fieldset> and <legend>.

Perhaps the single most important element for form accessibility and usability is the <label> element, which associates descriptive text with a form control.

To focus entirely on the purpose, syntax, and importance of <label>, we will remove the radio button <fieldset> example from index.html.

Why Remove Code?

  • Focus: Isolate the <label> element and its association methods (for/id).
  • Clarity: Start with a clean form container to demonstrate adding controls and their corresponding labels correctly.
