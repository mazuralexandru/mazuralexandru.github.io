395. <figure> & <figcaption>: Structure

Files Modified: None

The structural rules for <figure> and <figcaption> are important for validity:

  • <figcaption> Placement: The <figcaption> element, if present, must be the first or the last child element directly inside the <figure> tags. It cannot be placed in the middle of other content within the figure.

    <!-- CORRECT: figcaption first -->
<figure>
  <figcaption>Caption Text</figcaption>
  <img src="..." alt="...">
  <p>Optional related paragraph</p>
</figure>

<!-- CORRECT: figcaption last -->
<figure>
  <img src="..." alt="...">
  <p>Optional related paragraph</p>
  <figcaption>Caption Text</figcaption>
</figure>

<!-- INCORRECT: figcaption in the middle -->
<figure>
  <img src="..." alt="...">
  <figcaption>Caption Text</figcaption> <!-- Invalid placement -->
  <p>Optional related paragraph</p>
</figure>

  • Only One <figcaption>: A <figure> element can contain at most one <figcaption> element.

  • Figure Content: The <figure> element itself can contain various flow content besides the optional <figcaption>, such as images, code blocks, paragraphs, headings, etc., as long as they form part of the self-contained unit being captioned.

Adhering to this structure ensures your markup is valid and the relationship between the figure and its caption is correctly understood by browsers and assistive technologies.
