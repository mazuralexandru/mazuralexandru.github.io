440. background-position: Values (Keywords, Lengths, Percentages)

Files Modified: None

Let's clarify how the different value types for background-position work:

  • Keywords (top, bottom, left, right, center):
    • Align the corresponding edge or center of the background image with the corresponding edge or center of the background positioning area (usually the padding box).
    • top left aligns the image's top-left corner with the container's top-left padding edge.
    • center center aligns the center of the image with the center of the container.
    • bottom right aligns the image's bottom-right corner with the container's bottom-right padding edge.
  • Lengths (px, em, rem, etc.):
    • Specify an offset from the top-left edge of the background positioning area.
    • background-position: 20px 50px; places the top-left corner of the image 20px from the container's left edge and 50px from its top edge.
  • Percentages (%):
    • This is slightly less intuitive. A percentage value X% Y% aligns the point X% across and Y% down within the image with the corresponding point X% across and Y% down within the background positioning area.
    • 0% 0% = top left.
    • 100% 100% = bottom right.
    • 50% 50% = center center.
    • 25% 75% aligns the point 25% across / 75% down the image with the point 25% across / 75% down the container.

Keywords and lengths are often the most straightforward for positioning, while percentages are useful for relative alignment.
