145. Introduction to Links: The Anchor Element (<a>)

Welcome to Module 4, where we delve into one of the most fundamental building blocks of the World Wide Web: hyperlinks, often simply called links. Links are what make the web a "web," allowing users to navigate between pages, documents, and other resources.

The Anchor Element (<a>)

  • Definition: The primary HTML element used to create hyperlinks is the anchor element, represented by the <a> tag.
  • Analogy: Think of it as an anchor point in your document that connects to somewhere else.

We will explore its structure, attributes, and various uses throughout this module.
