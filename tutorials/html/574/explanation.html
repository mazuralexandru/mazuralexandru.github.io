574. Reset: Preparing for Legacy Embedding (<embed>, <object>)

We have thoroughly covered the native HTML5 elements for multimedia, <video> and <audio>, along with their crucial supporting elements <source> and <track>. These are the standard, modern approaches.

However, it's useful to be aware of older, more generic embedding elements, <embed> and <object>, primarily to understand legacy code or specific edge cases where they might still (rarely) appear. These elements were heavily used in the pre-HTML5 era, often requiring browser plugins like Flash.

To focus on these legacy elements, we will remove the <audio> example from index.html.

Why Remove Code?

  • Focus: Isolate the distinct syntax and historical context of <embed> and <object>.
  • Clean Slate: Provide an empty body container.
