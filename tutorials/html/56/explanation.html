56. Preparing for Thematic Breaks: Removing <br> Examples

We've covered the use and misuse of the <br> element. Now, to prepare for introducing another structural element, the horizontal rule (<hr>), let's remove the <br> examples from our index.html.

Why Remove Code?

  • Focus: To provide a clean separation between the concept of intra-paragraph line breaks (<br>) and inter-paragraph thematic breaks (<hr>).
  • Clarity: Resetting the paragraphs to simple, unbroken text blocks makes the effect of adding an <hr> between them more distinct and obvious.
  • Relevance: The specific line break examples (demonstration text, address) are not needed to understand the purpose of <hr>.

Code Change:

We have removed all <br> tags and updated the paragraph content in index.html.

<body>
  <h1>Main Page Title (h1)</h1>
  <p>This is the introductory paragraph. We will explore thematic breaks next.</p> <!-- Removed <br>, adjusted text -->
  <!-- Removed: <br>This text appears on a new line within the same paragraph. -->

  <p>This is another paragraph, providing separation for the thematic break example.</p> <!-- Removed address, adjusted text -->
  <!-- Removed address lines with <br> -->


  <!-- <p>This paragraph is temporarily commented out.</p> -->
</body>

✅ Observation:

  1. Confirm that index.html no longer contains any <br> tags. The paragraphs are now simple, single blocks of text.
  2. The browser preview should reflect this, showing the heading and two plain paragraphs below it, ready for the next element.

✨ Key Takeaway: Clearing out previous examples, especially when shifting between related but distinct concepts (like <br> vs <hr>), helps prevent confusion and keeps the learning path focused.
