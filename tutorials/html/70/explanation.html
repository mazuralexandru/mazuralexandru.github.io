70. <title>: Best Practices

Files Modified: None

Here are some tips for writing effective page titles:

  • Be Descriptive and Concise: Accurately summarize the page's main content or purpose. Aim for around 50-60 characters, as search engines often truncate longer titles in results.
  • Make it Unique: Each page on your site should have a unique title that distinguishes it from others. Avoid generic titles like "Page 2" or "Details".
  • Front-load Keywords (If Appropriate): Place the most important keywords near the beginning of the title, as users and search engines scan from left to right. Don't stuff keywords unnaturally, though.
  • Include Branding (Optional, Often Last): Consider adding your site or brand name, usually at the end, separated by a hyphen or pipe (e.g., About Us | CompanyName).
  • Consider the User: Write titles for humans first, search engines second. Make them informative and relevant to someone looking at a browser tab or search result.

Example progression:

  • Bad: Home
  • Okay: Welcome to My Website
  • Better: CompanyName - Official Website
  • Good: Affordable Web Design Services | CompanyName (If that's the page topic)
