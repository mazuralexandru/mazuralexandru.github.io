322. Deprecated <meta>: Keywords (name="keywords") - Practical Application & Best Practices

Given that major search engines ignore the <meta name="keywords"> tag, what are the practical recommendations for web developers?

Recommendation:

  • Generally, Omit It: For most new websites and pages targeting major search engines, it is safe and recommended to simply omit the <meta name="keywords"> tag entirely. It adds clutter to your <head> section without providing any significant SEO benefit.
  • Do Not Waste Time: Do not spend time researching, compiling, and maintaining keyword lists specifically for this tag. Your time is much better spent on creating valuable content, optimizing titles and descriptions, building quality links, and improving user experience.

Where to Focus SEO Efforts Instead:

  • High-Quality, Relevant Content: Create content that genuinely answers user questions and meets their needs.
  • Keyword Research (for Content): Research keywords to understand user search intent, but integrate those keywords naturally into your page content, headings (<h1>-<h6>), <title> tags, and <meta name="description">.
  • Compelling <title> Tags: Craft unique, descriptive titles that include primary keywords near the beginning.
  • Engaging <meta name="description">: Write unique summaries that accurately reflect the page and encourage clicks from SERPs.
  • Logical Heading Structure: Use headings (<h1>-<h6>) hierarchically to structure content clearly.
  • Image alt Text: Use descriptive alt text for images.
  • Internal Linking: Use descriptive anchor text for links between your own pages.
  • User Experience & Page Speed: Ensure your site is fast, mobile-friendly, and easy to navigate.
  • Quality Backlinks: Earn links from other reputable websites (a major off-page factor).

When Might You Include It?

  • If required by a specific, non-standard tool or platform (e.g., certain niche CMS plugins, internal search systems) that you know actively uses it. Check the documentation for that specific tool.
  • For historical reasons on very old sites (though removing it likely has no negative impact).
