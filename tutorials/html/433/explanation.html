433. background-image: Gradients

Files Modified: None

CSS gradients are generated images, allowing you to create smooth color transitions without needing an actual image file. They are treated as values for background-image.

  • linear-gradient(): Creates a gradient along a straight line.
    • Syntax: linear-gradient([<angle> | to <side-or-corner>]?, <color-stop-list>)
    • Direction: Can be an angle (45deg, 0deg is to top) or keywords (to top, to bottom left, etc.). Defaults to to bottom.
    • Color Stops: Comma-separated list of colors, optionally with positions (percentages or lengths). Colors are blended evenly between stops if positions aren't specified.
    • Example: linear-gradient(to right, red, yellow) (Red on left fading to yellow on right)
    • Example: linear-gradient(135deg, blue, green 40%, orange) (Diagonal, blue to green stopping at 40%, then green to orange)
  • radial-gradient(): Creates a gradient radiating outwards from a central point.
    • Syntax: radial-gradient([<shape> || <size>]? [at <position>]?, <color-stop-list>)
    • Shape: circle or ellipse (default).
    • Size: Keywords like closest-side, farthest-corner (default), or explicit lengths/percentages.
    • Position: Keywords (center, top, etc.) or lengths/percentages, defining the gradient's center. Defaults to center.
    • Example: radial-gradient(circle at center, yellow, red) (Yellow circle fading to red)
  • Repeating Gradients: repeating-linear-gradient() and repeating-radial-gradient() create patterns by repeating the defined color stops.

Gradients are incredibly versatile for backgrounds, button effects, overlays, and more.
